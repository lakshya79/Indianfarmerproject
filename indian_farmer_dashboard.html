03

Course promotion: is it working?

Only 4 videos in the 90-day set carry an explicit course CTA. None of them beat the channel's long-form subscriber-conversion average (4.24 per 1,000 views), and one — a Shorts CTA relying purely on a pinned comment link — pulled a CTR of just 0.94% against a channel norm of ~6.5%.

05

Three things to act on

01 — Rebalance the upload mix

Long-form converts subscribers 4.8× better per view than Shorts (4.24 vs 0.88 per 1,000). Shift a meaningful share of production budget from volume Shorts toward 1–2 more "A to Z" long-form guides a week — they're doing the real growth work.

02 — Stop launching dedicated course videos

All 4 course-CTA videos underperform the catalog; the weakest scored 0.94% CTR and the live-launch video drew only 4,103 views. Retire standalone course videos — embed the CTA inside proven high-performing long-form formats instead, where audiences already convert.

03 — Fix the channel page

Channel-page CTR is 1.7% against 8.42% from Browse features and 5.87% from Search — the weakest click-through of any major surface. Refresh the channel trailer and featured-video thumbnails; this is the first impression for new visitors.
